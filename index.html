About the Project
Science-driven governance
for a resilient water future

The Jaffna Peninsula relies heavily on a fragile freshwater lens overlying a saline aquifer, increasingly threatened by rising demand, saline intrusion, agricultural pollution, and climate variability. This project, led by IWMI with support from the UK's FCDO and the Northern Provincial Council, adopts a multi-pronged, science-driven approach to develop multisectoral pathways for sustainable groundwater management.

By combining real-time monitoring across 15 stations, school-based citizen science in 22 schools, community engagement with 225 farmers, and a Multi-Stakeholder Platform, we are building the most comprehensive evidence base ever assembled for Jaffna's groundwater system.
Mission

Multisectoral Pathways for Improved Management

To identify multisectoral pathways for improved management of Jaffna's groundwater resources — mitigating risks to quantity and quality, ensuring resilient agriculture and livelihoods, and supporting climate change adaptation and environmental protection. Aligned with SDG 6 (Clean Water) and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

Vision

Resilient Groundwater, Empowered Communities

A Jaffna Peninsula where groundwater is sustainably managed, supporting secure livelihoods, climate-resilient communities, and an environmentally sustainable future through science-driven governance, transparent data, and empowered communities.
