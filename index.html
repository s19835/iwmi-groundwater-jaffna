About the Project
Science-driven governance
for a resilient water future

Groundwater is the primary water source for the Jaffna Peninsula, yet it remains increasingly stressed by over-extraction, pollution, governance gaps, and climate variability. This project, led by IWMI with support from the UK's FCDO, adopts a research-driven, multi-institutional approach to better understand and sustainably manage this fragile resource.

By combining scientific monitoring, community-led citizen science and policy engagement, we create the most comprehensive evidence base ever assembled for Jaffna's groundwater system.
Mission

Effective Pathways for Sustainable Management

To identify and promote effective pathways for improved groundwater management by addressing threats to water quantity and quality — safeguarding sustainable agriculture, enhancing climate resilience, and upholding environmental integrity through scientific research and collaborative action.

Vision

Resilient Groundwater, Empowered Communities

A Jaffna Peninsula where groundwater is sustainably managed, supporting secure livelihoods, climate-resilient communities, and an environmentally sustainable future through science-driven governance, transparent data, and empowered communities.
